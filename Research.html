Nano proteomic sample preperation for single cell proteomics

Protocol

nPOP is a droplet based method that for single cell proteomic sample preperation. We see nPOP as a conceptual framework moreso than a specific workflow. In this post I will talk about why we designed nPOP, how we nPOP was developed with several goals in mind.

  • 1) Miniaturize sample preperation volumes to several nanoliters. This has an number of benefits. It enables cleaner samples by reducing the amout of reagents needed to prepare a single cell. It also enables higher sample preperation throughput by allowing users to prepare a larger number of samples with less reagents. Lastly, it can reduce sample preperation losses by exposing samples to less surface area.
  • 2) Fully automate the sample preperation. This can remove batch effects related to user error, increasing data consistency while also allowing for increased throughput by removing manual pipetting steps.
  • 3) Create a flexible workflow that allow users to maximize the number of samples simultaneously prepared and can accomidate many different experimental designs.

To achieve these goals, we took a unique approach to sample preperation, abandoning familiar staples of mass spectrometry sample preperation such as tube and wells. In our approach, single cells are prepared in nanoliter droplets on the surface of an unpatterned glass slide with a flourocarbon coating. In these droplets, we lyse cells, digest proteins to peptides, label cells for mutiplexing, and pool samples into a set. The samples are dispensed into a 384 well plate where they can be picked up by most standard autosamplers. We use the Ultimate 3000 from Thermo.

To perform the sample preperation, we use the CellenONE liquid handling and cell dispensing platform made by Scienion.
